Control theory ought to underlie analog computing the way Logic underlies digital computing. The fact that humans are capable of digital reasoning and yet run as analog machines tells us that there must be a connection between the two, at least at the level where our digital thought processes are implemented as ion flows.

At present John Lygeros is talking about Control theory in an introductory way for those of us who either have forgotten all our analysis or never knew any in the first place. The present long-term aim of the group is to understand some results in the theory of differential games.
